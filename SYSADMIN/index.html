What is aquaponics?

Aquaponics is a method of growing plants in water and using this as a habitat for fish.


Components of a basic aquaponics setup
  • Fishtank (aquarium)
  • Solid filters
  • Biological Filter
  • Grow Bed
  • Sump Tank
Why grow food this way?
  • Aquaponic uses less water
  • Lessen your time for fishkeepings
  • Completely Organic
  • Food can can be grown anywhere
Things to check in aquaponics system
  • Temperature
  • Insects
  • pH Level
  • Ammonia & Nitrate Level

Types of Aquaponic Systems

Media Based Aquaponics System

Media-based systems, also known as floods and drainage, are the most popular aquaponics systems popular in home refurbishment, backyard housing systems, and commercial farms. In media-based systems, plants grow on plant substrates such as gravel and swollen clay pebbles. Media filter waste containing ammonia and solid waste. The media-based system design is simple, space-saving, and low-cost, making it suitable for aquaponics beginners.

Raft System

Raft system also known as deep water culture or floating systems, plants are cultivated on a raft board (polystyrene or foam board) that floats on the water of the raft bed. Nutrient-filled water flows from the aquarium through a filtration process into the tank where the plants grow and then back into the aquarium. In most cases, the raft tank is separated from the aquarium. Many commercial aquaponics farms use this system because the plants can grow faster and get more harvest.

Nutrient Film Technique( NFT)

NFT are popular in commercial aquaponics because they are more practical than other aquaponics techniques. This technique is even more useful in urban areas where space and food production are an issue. However, it is expensive to set up and is not suitable for locations with insufficient access to the supplier.

Hybrid Aquaponics System

The hybrid aquaponics system is a combination of several types of aquaponics systems. Most commercial aquaponics use hybrid systems because of their efficiency and large space utilization. You can combine the systems to suit your needs. There are several approaches to aquaponics systems, all of which work well depending on how the system is designed, built, and maintained.
